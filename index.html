The Ethos Pathos and Logos of Sir Ken Robinson's Ted talk

The TED Talk Itself

In his 20 minute TED Talk Ken Robinson discusses the ways in which education actually stifles creativity in an engaging, entertaining, and educational manner. Give it a watch!

The focus of my analysis

Ethos

Ethos is an appeal to the speakers authority on the subject
When a speaker uses ethos they are presenting their credibility on the subject. They may give examples of work experience, awards, education, or other relevant life experience to convince the listener they know what they're talking about.

Pathos

Pathos is an appeal to the audiences emotions
When a speaker uses pathos they are trying to illicit an emotional response from the listener. They may speak about relatable experiences, play on our sympathies with a sad tale, or try to make us joyful with an uplifting one.

Logos

Logos is an appeal to logic, reason, or fact
When a speaker employs logos they are arguing with fact and reason. A speaker might make a logical argument or present some statistical data. Logos shows the listener the facts to support the speakers argument.
